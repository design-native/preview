Net-Zero Layer 3 Platform

Net-Zero Consensus

The Net-Zero Consensus is a mechanism designed to transparently manage and coordinate carbon emissions, reductions, and carbon credits.
It leverages blockchain technology to reliably verify and record carbon credits (emission rights and offset credits), reduction activities, and the burning process.

  • Carbon Credit Burning Consensus

    1. Carbon emission offset and carbon credit burning
    2. Carbon emission verification through burn request
    3. Thorough verification process via rollup
    4. Permanently recorded net-zero status on the blockchain

  • Carbon Reduction Consensus

    1. Accurate identification of emission sources
    2. Analysis and verification of reduction activities
    3. Carbon credit issuance upon achievement of reduction targets

Carbon Emission Data Tokenization

To tokenize centralized carbon emission data, the Notary Oracle verifies the data and
certifies it through a multi-signature process between the sender and receiver, ensuring the reliability of the external data.

The Notary Oracle tokenizes external carbon emission data using calculators from certified institutions.

Carbon Offset Data Tokenization

To tokenize centralized carbon offset data (carbon absorption and carbon reduction), the Notary Oracle verifies the data and certifies it through a multi-signature process between the data sender and receiver, ensuring the reliability of the external data.

The Notary Oracle tokenizes external carbon absorption and reduction data authenticated by certified institutions.

Net-Zero Consensus Algorithm for Carbon Emission and Offset Tokenization

An Integrated Framework for Achieving Net-Zero by Aggregating Carbon Emission and Offset Data and Implementing a Consensus Algorithm for Carbon Credit Burning and Reduction through a Notarized Multi-Signature Process.
